My Research

I focus my research on investigating the neural basis of states of consciousness. I have started with the study of mind wandering and meditation using mainly EEG. I then used fMRI to study the state of hypnosis. I am now moving on combining EEG and TMS to study the neural basis of vivid mental imagery, that I consider as being a core element of mind wandering, meditation and hypnosis.

To study these states of consciousness, I try to combine both cutting-edge approaches in neuroimaging and collection of first-person data.

My Curriculum

    Since March 2016, I am a Marie Sklodowska-Curie post-doctoral fellow in the Psychology department , at the University of Plymouth (UK). I work on studying the neural basis of vivid mental imagery , in collaboration with Pr. Giorgio Ganis.

    From April 2013 to March 2015, I was a post-doctoral fellow at the University of Geneva, Switzerland, in the Laboratory for Neurology and Imaging of Cognition. I worked with Pr. Patrik Vuilleumier to study the hypnotic state and its effects on attention and empathy using fMRI and EEG.

    I defended my PhD in September 2012, at the CerCO-CNRS UMR5549 in Toulouse, France. I worked under supervision of Pr.Arnaud Delorme and studied the EEG correlates of meditation and mind-wandering.
